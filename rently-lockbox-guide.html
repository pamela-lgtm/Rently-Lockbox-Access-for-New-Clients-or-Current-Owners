Lockbox Guide

Everything you need to know to register your phone with Rently, place your house key inside the lockbox, and complete the simple handoff that lets us begin marketing your property.

Important
Until you physically place the key inside the lockbox, no one can enter your property. You stay in full control of when access begins.

The Process

Four Simple Steps

From start to finish, the whole process takes about ten minutes. Here's exactly what to do.

01
Register your mobile device with Rently

Each mobile device that will need to open the lockbox must be registered separately. Once we've added you into the Rently system, you'll automatically receive a text message and email from Rently containing your phone verification code.

Have that code handy before you begin.

From the device you wish to register, call:

(888) 889-8357
Follow the voice prompts
  1. Follow the prompts to register your phone number.
  2. When prompted for a code, enter the verification code Rently sent you, followed by #
  3. When prompted for a serial number, enter the serial number of the lockbox, followed by #
Multiple devices If you'd like more than one device registered (a spouse's phone, a co-owner's phone, a family member helping out), repeat this process from each additional device. Each device must be registered individually using the same verification code.
Tip from Linda If you don't see the verification code text or email within a few minutes, check your spam folder, then reach out to Linda. She can confirm the code was sent and resend if needed.
02
Place ONE key in the lockbox

Inside the Rently lockbox, place one (1) house key only. This is the working key our team and approved showing agents will use to access the property.

Important Do not place any additional keys, remotes, or access devices inside the lockbox. The lockbox is designed to hold a single key for security and operational reasons.
Tip from Linda One key is enough as long as it allows entry. Any additional keys will be discussed with Linda.
03
Leave all remaining items on the kitchen counter

Please leave the following items together on the kitchen counter. We'll inventory and secure them when we arrive for the next visit.

Items to leave on the counter
  • Any additional house keys
  • Garage door remotes
  • Gate remotes, cards, or fobs
  • HOA amenity access cards or fobs (pool, clubhouse, fitness center, etc.)
  • Mailbox keys
  • Any pool keys or community access devices
  • Any manuals, warranties, or appliance documentation, if available
Don't worry if some don't apply If any of these items don't apply to your property, simply leave what does apply. There's no need to track down items that don't exist.
04
Close the lockbox

Once the single key is placed inside, close the lockbox completely to ensure it is secured.

Give it a gentle tug to confirm it's locked — there should be no give, no rattle. If something feels off, open it again and reseat the key.

You're done Once the lockbox is closed, send Linda a quick note letting her know everything is in place. We'll take it from there.

A Note on Security

How Rently Protects Your Property

One of the reasons we use Rently — rather than a traditional combination lockbox — is the layered identity verification it requires of every prospective tenant before they're allowed inside.

Photo ID required

Every prospect must upload a valid government-issued photo ID before they can request a self-showing code.

Live selfie match

Rently requires a live selfie taken in the moment, which is matched against the uploaded ID before access is granted.

Credit card on file

A valid credit card must be provided. This adds another layer of identity verification and accountability for everyone who enters your home.

Every showing logged

Verandah sees a complete record of who accessed your property, when they arrived, and how long they stayed.

Frequently Asked

Common Questions

I didn't receive my Rently verification code — what do I do?

Verify the code was sent to the correct phone and email by checking with Linda. Don't forget to look in your spam folder — Rently emails sometimes land there.

If the code still hasn't arrived, Linda can have it resent in under a minute.

Can I register more than one phone?

Yes. Each mobile device that will need to open the lockbox must be registered separately by repeating the same registration call from that device. Use the same verification code for each one.

Many owners register a spouse, co-owner, or family member's phone in addition to their own.

Where do I find the lockbox serial number?

The serial number is on the lockbox itself — typically a small label or marking on the front or side. Linda will also include the serial number in the email or text she sends you.

Can someone else (a friend, family member, or contractor) place the key for me?

Yes — and many owners choose this option, especially if they live out of state. The person helping will need to register their own phone with Rently using the same process. Just let Linda know who will be doing it, and she'll make sure the verification flow gets to the right person.

What if the lockbox won't open after I've registered?

Confirm you registered the correct phone number and entered the correct serial number during registration. If something doesn't match, the lockbox won't accept your access attempt.

Call Linda directly. She can verify your registration and walk you through the process in real time.

Once the key is inside, can I still access my own property?

Yes. You're registered with Rently, so you can use your own phone to open the lockbox anytime you'd like to visit. You also keep a copy of your house key — only one key goes inside the lockbox.

Is the lockbox weatherproof?

Yes. The Rently lockbox is rated for outdoor use through Florida weather — rain, sun, and humidity will not affect its operation.

What happens to the lockbox after a tenant moves in?

The lockbox is removed once the tenant has been given their keys at move-in. From that point forward, your property is no longer accessed via Rently — the tenant has their own keys.

Will the lockbox damage my front door?

No. It hangs from the door handle or a nearby secured location and does not require any drilling, screws, or adhesive. When it's removed, there's no trace it was ever there.
